About

My journey from systems thinking to impactful design.

  • 2009-2013

    Building a Foundation

    At Mercer University, I developed a strong foundation in chemistry and biology, fueled by a passion for solving real-world problems. Paul Polak's 'The Business Solution to Poverty' planted the seed for my interest in design as a tool for change.

  • 2013-2015

    Designing Solutions in Mozambique

    As a Peace Corps volunteer, I implemented drip irrigation systems, improving food security and empowering communities. This hands-on work highlighted the importance of design, collaboration, and systemic thinking.

  • 2015-2016

    Navigating Complexity in Dubai

    At Bloomberg in Dubai, I helped expand foreign exchange and bond markets in Africa while consulting on sustainable agriculture projects. I learned to navigate corruption, complexity, and the challenges of systemic inefficiencies.

  • 2016-2023

    Building Systems and Driving Transparency

    In New York, I built data analytics infrastructure at Bloomberg and later at VTS, leveraging tools like DBT and Miro to reduce complexity and drive transparency in complex systems.
